Story part 1

The story starts with a boy whose name is Ajay. Ajay had been troubled for a few days because his eyes would open at exactly 12:50 at night. He felt as if someone was calling his name. “Ajay… Ajay…” The voice seemed familiar to him, but whenever he woke up, no one was there. Naturally, he grew restless. The next morning, during breakfast, his mother noticed his unease and asked him: Mother: “What happened, Ajay? You look worried.” Ajay: “I don’t know, Mom. For the past few nights, I haven’t been able to sleep properly. Every night at 12:50, my eyes open. It feels like someone is calling me.” Mother: “It’s nothing. You must have seen a dream. Try to sleep early at night and spend less time on your phone.” His mother dismissed it as just his imagination, but Ajay knew something strange was happening to him. His holidays had ended, and he thought perhaps this odd experience would stop now. After all, schoolwork and fun with friends at the hostel would keep him busy. But things didn’t go as he had hoped. The days passed normally, yet the nights felt unsettling. At the hostel, Ajay shared his room with his friend Sahil. Sahil was his only close companion, an introvert who didn’t talk to anyone else and had no other friends. That night, the two had dinner together and then went to bed. After a while, both of them drifted into sleep… That night, Ajay’s eyes opened once again. When he looked at the clock, fear gripped him — it was 12:50. Suddenly, he felt as though someone was watching him from the corner of the room. As he turned his gaze, his heart pounded harder, for he saw a shadow standing there. The figure had glowing red eyes and long, sharp nails. Before Ajay could scream or run, the shadow rushed toward him. Terrified, he jolted awake with a loud cry. His friend Sahil, startled by the noise, quickly got up and handed him some water. Ajay explained everything, but Sahil dismissed it, saying it was only a dream. Eventually, both of them went back to sleep. The next day passed as usual — schoolwork, fun with friends, and evening outings. Ajay often went out with his friends in the evenings, but Sahil preferred to stay back in the hostel room. Ajay thought maybe Sahil simply didn’t enjoy going out, but the truth was something else entirely. When Ajay returned to the room, he saw Sahil sitting quietly. Ajay: “Hey bro, what are you doing?” Sahil: “Nothing much, just finishing my homework.” Ajay: “What’s the point of completing homework? You never even get your notebook checked by the teacher.” Sahil: “I know… I’m just scared to go in front of the teacher.” Ajay: “Then I’ll get your notebook checked for you.” Sahil: “It won’t matter. The teacher won’t check my notebook anyway.” Ajay: “Why? What do you mean?” Sahil: “Nothing… it’s just that they’ve never seen me, and they never will.” Ajay: “What does that mean?” Sahil: “It means I’ll never get my notebook checked. Forget it. Let’s just eat. Bring the food here.” Ajay: “Sometimes you should eat with me and the others in the hall.” Sahil: “No, you just bring it here.” Ajay: “Okay.” The two of them ate together in the room and then went to sleep. Today again at 12:50, Ajay hears a voice. He thinks maybe Sahil is speaking: “Ajay… Ajay…” But when he looks, he realizes Sahil is actually sleeping. He looks near the room’s door and gets frightened. He sees a shadow moving under the door. Ajay wakes his friend and tells him everything. Sahil listens carefully, and just then, the door is knocked. Ajay: “I told you this isn’t my imagination, look near the door!” Sahil: “Don’t be scared, it must be some hostel student playing a prank.” But Sahil was also scared. To overcome the fear, he slowly goes and opens the door. He gets terrified because the corridor was empty, only a dim light was glowing. But in the corridor there was an unusual coldness that made Sahil restless. A strange smell was spread there. Sahil: “Maybe you’re right… now even I feel something strange is happening.” Ajay: “I told you earlier.” Sahil: “I’m going home tomorrow itself. You should also take leave and go home.” After that, both somehow spend the night in fear. In the morning, they take leave and go home. When Ajay tells his parents everything, they get anxious — because they knew something that they never wanted Ajay to find out.
