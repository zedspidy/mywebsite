Story part 3

Ajay had started living in constant fear. He rarely stepped out of the house and spoke less, even with people he liked. One day he tried calling his friend Sahil, but the phone was switched off. He thought he should go directly to Sahil’s house to meet him. When he reached, he rang the bell but no one came. After a while, a lady appeared — she seemed to be Sahil’s mother. When she saw Ajay, she asked who he was. Ajay: I am Sahil’s friend. Sahil’s Mom: Are you joking? Ajay: How is this a joke? I’ve been his friend for the past year. Yes, I admit he doesn’t talk to many people, but with me… Sahil’s Mom: Are you crazy? You’re saying you’ve been his friend for the past year, but Sahil died 10 years ago. Hearing this, Ajay felt the ground slip beneath his feet. If Sahil had died 10 years ago, then with whom had Ajay been living in the same room for the past year? Ajay tried to convince her by describing Sahil’s habits, but his mother said: Yes, he was an introvert, spoke less with people, but he went down the wrong path from which he never returned. Ajay: What kind of path did Sahil go down? Sahil’s Mom: He kept himself away from people, and for that he turned to intoxication. One day, because of drugs, he fell from the roof and died. Ajay: How did you know he was taking drugs? Sahil’s Mom: At the time of his death, there was white powder on his mouth, and it smelled like drugs. Hearing all this, Ajay was completely confused. He went to the hostel and asked the owner about Sahil. The owner said: Apart from you, no one lived in that room. You used to talk to yourself, so no one wanted to stay with you. Since you paid rent on time, I didn’t evict you. After hearing this, Ajay’s mind began to spin. He started feeling that he was losing his sanity.
